## Getting Started

### Logging In
1. Navigate to the portal URL
2. Click **"Login with Singpass"** or **"Login with CorpPass"** (for corporate users)
3. Complete the authentication process
4. You'll be directed to your dashboard

### Your Role
As a doctor, you are responsible for:
- Reviewing medical examination submissions created by nurses
- Verifying accuracy and completeness of medical data
- Approving submissions for government submission
- Rejecting incomplete or inaccurate submissions with clear reasons
- Ensuring compliance with regulatory requirements

## Reviewing Submissions

### Opening a Submission

1. From the **"Pending Review"** tab
2. Find the submission to review
3. Click the **"Review"** button (eye icon)
4. The submission details page opens

### Submission Details Page Layout

**Left Panel - Examination Details:**
Shows all medical information in sections:

1. **Patient Information**
   - NRIC/FIN, Name, Date of Birth
   - Gender, Contact details
   - Examination date

2. **Exam-Specific Content**
   - Varies by examination type (see below)

3. **Remarks**
   - Nurse's notes and observations
   - Internal comments

**Right Panel - Submission Metadata:**
- Status badge
- Submission ID
- Examination category
- Created by (nurse)
- Creation date
- Submitted date

### Review Checklist

Before approving any submission, verify:

✅ **Patient Identification**
- NRIC/FIN format is correct
- Name matches expected format
- Date of birth is reasonable

✅ **Completeness**
- All required fields are filled
- No missing data or "N/A" where inappropriate
- Test results are recorded

✅ **Accuracy**
- Data makes clinical sense
- No obvious errors or typos
- Measurements are within reasonable ranges

✅ **Clinical Appropriateness**
- Findings are consistent
- Any abnormalities are documented
- Required tests were performed

✅ **Regulatory Compliance**
- Meets authority requirements
- All declarations completed
- Proper certification obtained

## Reviewing by Exam Type

### 6-Monthly MDW/FMW Examinations

**Key Sections to Review:**

1. **Test Results**
   - Pregnancy Test: Positive/Negative
   - Syphilis Test: Positive/Negative
   - HIV Test: Positive/Negative (if required)
   - Chest X-Ray: Positive/Negative (if required)

2. **Physical Examination**
   - Suspicious injuries: Yes/No
   - Unintentional weight loss: Yes/No
   - Police report status (if applicable)

3. **Remarks**
   - Review nurse's notes
   - Check for any concerns flagged

**What to Check:**
- All required tests are performed
- Positive results are flagged clearly
- Police report documented if injuries present
- No conflicting information

**Common Issues:**
- Missing test results
- Unclear positive/negative designation
- Missing police report when required
- No remarks for abnormal findings

### ICA Examinations (PR/Student Pass/LTVP)

**Key Sections to Review:**

1. **Medical History**
   - Past medical conditions
   - Current medications
   - Allergies and reactions

2. **Physical Examination**
   - Height, Weight, BMI (auto-calculated)
   - Blood Pressure
   - Visual acuity
   - General physical findings

3. **Test Results**
   - As per ICA requirements
   - Chest X-ray (if applicable)
   - Blood tests (if applicable)

4. **Declaration**
   - Patient certification completed
   - Medical practitioner declaration

**What to Check:**
- Medical history is complete
- BMI calculation is correct
- Blood pressure is reasonable
- Visual acuity recorded properly
- All required tests completed
- Declarations are signed

**Common Issues:**
- Incomplete medical history
- Missing test results
- Unreasonable vital signs
- Missing patient certification

### Driving Examinations (TP/TP_LTA/LTA)

**Key Sections to Review:**

1. **Medical Declaration by Patient**
   - List of conditions patient has declared
   - Patient certification confirmed
   - Declaration statement acknowledged

2. **Medical History of Patient**
   - Relevant medical conditions
   - Patient certification confirmed
   - History declarations complete

3. **General Medical Examination**
   - **Cardiovascular Assessment:**
     - Blood Pressure (systolic/diastolic)
     - Pulse rate
     - S1/S2 heart sounds
     - Murmurs
   
   - **Vision Assessment:**
     - Optical aids required (Yes/No)
     - Visual acuity
     - Near vision (Right Eye, Left Eye)
     - Distant vision (Right Eye, Left Eye)
     - Colour vision
   
   - **Physical Examination:**
     - General appearance
     - Musculoskeletal system
     - Neurological examination
     - Other relevant findings

4. **AMT (Abbreviated Mental Test)** - For TP/TP_LTA only
   - 10 questions with answers
   - Automatic scoring (0-10)
   - Pass/Fail result (≥7 = Pass)
   - Warning if score < 7

5. **Overall Result of Medical Examination**
   - Fit to drive: Yes/No/Yes with conditions
   - If Yes with conditions: Details of restrictions
   - Medical practitioner declaration
   - Your certification

**What to Check:**
- Patient declarations are complete
- All certifications confirmed
- Vital signs are within acceptable ranges
- Vision meets driving standards
- AMT score is ≥7 (if applicable)
- Physical exam findings are documented
- Your assessment is appropriate
- Any conditions/restrictions are clearly stated

**Vision Standards (General Guidelines):**
- Visual acuity: Typically 6/12 or better
- Colour vision: Must be adequate
- Field of vision: No significant defects
- Check specific requirements for each license type

**AMT Interpretation:**
- Score ≥7: Pass (cognitively fit)
- Score <7: Fail (possible cognitive impairment)
- Consider specialist referral if failed

**Common Issues:**
- Missing patient certifications
- Incomplete vision testing
- AMT not administered when required
- Unclear fitness determination
- Conditions not specified clearly
- Missing measurements

**Important Notes:**
- For TP/TP_LTA: AMT is mandatory for older drivers
- Vision testing must include both near and distant
- Document any optical aids used
- Specify restrictions clearly (e.g., "Daytime driving only")

### Work Permit Medical

**Key Sections to Review:**
- Similar to MDW/FMW
- Check employment-specific requirements
- Verify industry-specific tests

### Aged Drivers Examination

**Key Sections to Review:**
- Cognitive assessment results
- Vision and hearing tests
- Physical mobility assessment
- Reaction time evaluation
- Overall fitness determination

## Approving Submissions

### When to Approve

Approve a submission when:
- All information is complete and accurate
- Clinical findings are appropriate
- No errors or inconsistencies found
- Meets regulatory requirements
- You are satisfied with the examination

### How to Approve

1. Review the submission thoroughly
2. Scroll to the bottom of the page
3. Read the **Declaration** statement
4. Click the **"Approve Submission"** button
5. Confirm in the dialog box
6. Success message appears

### What Happens After Approval

- Status changes to **"Approved"** ✅
- Submission is marked with your name and MCR number
- Report is generated for government submission
- Nurse is notified
- Cannot be edited or reversed

### Your Declaration

By approving, you certify:
- You have examined and identified the patient
- Patient presented valid identification
- All answers are correct to best of your knowledge
- You take professional responsibility for the submission

## Rejecting Submissions

### When to Reject

Reject a submission when:
- Information is incomplete
- Data contains errors
- Clinical findings are inconsistent
- Required tests are missing
- Patient identification issues
- Does not meet regulatory standards
- Needs clarification from nurse

### How to Reject

1. Review the submission and identify issues
2. Scroll to the bottom of the page
3. Click the **"Reject Submission"** button
4. A dialog box appears
5. Enter a **clear, specific rejection reason**
6. Click **"Confirm Rejection"**
7. Success message appears

### Writing Good Rejection Reasons

✅ **GOOD Examples:**
```
"Blood pressure reading of 220/140 is extremely high and needs verification. 
Please recheck and confirm this value or provide context."

"AMT score shows 6/10 but patient answered only 5 questions correctly based 
on the recorded responses. Please re-administer the test."

"Patient NRIC format appears incorrect (S12345A). Please verify the complete 
9-character NRIC including checksum digit."

"Pregnancy test result not recorded. This is mandatory for MDW examination."

"Vision test shows near vision only. Please complete distant vision testing 
for both eyes as required for driving license."
```

❌ **POOR Examples:**
```
"Wrong data" - Too vague
"Fix this" - Not specific
"See me" - Doesn't document the issue
"Incomplete" - Doesn't say what's incomplete
```

### Best Practices for Rejections

1. **Be Specific**
   - State exactly what is wrong
   - Reference specific fields or sections
   - Cite requirements if applicable

2. **Be Professional**
   - Use respectful language
   - Focus on the issue, not the person
   - Provide guidance for correction

3. **Be Clear**
   - Avoid medical jargon if possible
   - Use simple, direct language
   - Number multiple issues if needed

4. **Be Educational**
   - Help the nurse learn from mistakes
   - Reference guidelines when appropriate
   - Suggest correct approach

### What Happens After Rejection

- Status changes to **"Rejected"** ❌
- Nurse receives notification with your reason
- Nurse can view the reason in submission details
- Nurse can **reopen** the submission
- Nurse makes corrections and resubmits
- You will review again when resubmitted

### Multiple Rejection Cycles

If a submission is rejected multiple times:
- Consider direct communication with the nurse
- Provide additional training if needed
- Document patterns for quality improvement

## Common Workflows

### Workflow 1: Standard Review & Approval

```
1. Log in to portal
   ↓
2. Check "Pending Review" tab
   ↓
3. Select a submission
   ↓
4. Review all sections carefully
   ↓
5. Verify completeness & accuracy
   ↓
6. Check clinical appropriateness
   ↓
7. Read declaration statement
   ↓
8. Click "Approve Submission"
   ↓
9. Confirm approval
   ↓
10. Move to next submission
```

### Workflow 2: Rejection & Correction Cycle

```
1. Review submission
   ↓
2. Identify issues/errors
   ↓
3. Click "Reject Submission"
   ↓
4. Write specific rejection reason
   ↓
5. Confirm rejection
   ↓
6. Nurse receives notification
   ↓
7. Nurse reopens & corrects
   ↓
8. Submission returns to your queue
   ↓
9. Review corrections
   ↓
10. Approve if corrected
```

### Workflow 3: Batch Review Session

For efficient processing of multiple submissions:

1. **Prepare Your Workspace**
   - Clear dedicated time
   - Minimize interruptions
   - Have reference materials ready

2. **Organize Your Queue**
   - Sort by exam type (batch similar types)
   - Or sort by urgency
   - Or sort by creation date (oldest first)

3. **Review Systematically**
   - Complete one full review before starting next
   - Use same review checklist for each
   - Maintain consistent standards

4. **Document Issues**
   - Note common errors for training
   - Flag quality concerns
   - Track rejection patterns

5. **Take Breaks**
   - Avoid fatigue-related errors
   - Maintain clinical judgment quality
   - Stay alert and focused

### Workflow 4: Driver Medical Reviews

For TP/TP_LTA/LTA examinations:

1. **Review Patient Declarations**
   - Check all medical conditions disclosed
   - Verify patient certification

2. **Review Medical History**
   - Assess relevant conditions
   - Verify patient certification

3. **Assess Physical Examination**
   - Verify all measurements recorded
   - Check vision testing (critical)
   - Review cardiovascular assessment

4. **Evaluate AMT** (if administered)
   - Verify all 10 questions answered
   - Check score calculation
   - Confirm pass/fail determination
   - Consider clinical context if borderline

5. **Make Fitness Determination**
   - Fit to drive (unconditional)
   - Fit with conditions (specify clearly)
   - Not fit to drive (document reasons)

6. **Complete Declaration**
   - Ensure all certifications complete
   - Verify your declaration
   - Approve or reject accordingly

## Clinical Guidelines

### General Principles

1. **Patient Safety First**
   - Never approve questionable submissions
   - When in doubt, reject with clear guidance
   - Prioritize public safety (especially for driving exams)

2. **Regulatory Compliance**
   - Know requirements for each exam type
   - Stay updated on guideline changes
   - Document thoroughly

3. **Professional Standards**
   - Maintain high clinical standards
   - Exercise independent judgment
   - Take professional responsibility seriously

### Exam-Specific Guidelines

#### MDW/FMW Examinations

**MOM Requirements:**
- Pregnancy test: Mandatory
- Syphilis test: Mandatory
- HIV test: When indicated
- Chest X-ray: When indicated
- Physical abuse screening: Required

**Red Flags:**
- Positive pregnancy test
- Positive syphilis test
- Positive HIV test
- Signs of abuse or injury
- Significant weight loss

**Action Items:**
- Document all positive results
- Ensure appropriate follow-up
- Verify police reporting if abuse suspected

#### ICA Examinations

**ICA Requirements:**
- Complete medical history
- Thorough physical examination
- Required laboratory tests
- Chest X-ray (for certain conditions)
- Specific disease screening

**Red Flags:**
- Communicable diseases
- Serious chronic conditions
- Incomplete vaccination history
- Abnormal chest X-ray

**Action Items:**
- Document all significant findings
- Recommend specialist referral if needed
- Ensure patient understands results

#### Driving Examinations

**LTA/TP Requirements:**
- Vision standards must be met
- Cardiovascular stability
- Cognitive function (AMT for older drivers)
- Physical capability to control vehicle
- No disqualifying conditions

**Disqualifying Conditions:**
- Severe visual impairment
- Uncontrolled epilepsy
- Significant cognitive impairment
- Recent stroke or heart attack
- Severe musculoskeletal limitations

**Conditional Approval:**
- Corrective lenses required
- Daytime driving only
- Specific vehicle modifications
- Limited duration license
- Regular review required

**Red Flags:**
- AMT score < 7
- Blood pressure > 180/110
- Visual acuity < 6/12
- Recent serious medical event
- Patient on sedating medications

**Action Items:**
- Clearly document restrictions
- Recommend specialist review if needed
- Consider shorter license validity
- Document clinical reasoning

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

**Problem: Cannot see any pending submissions**
- **Solution:** Check if you're assigned to any clinics
- Verify nurses are assigning submissions to you
- Contact administrator to check assignments

**Problem: Submission data seems incomplete**
- **Solution:** Reject with specific items needed
- Contact nurse if urgent clarification needed
- Do not approve incomplete submissions

**Problem: Patient data seems incorrect**
- **Solution:** Reject and request verification
- Ask nurse to confirm source documents
- Never approve questionable identification

**Problem: Cannot approve submission (button disabled)**
- **Solution:** Check submission status (may already be reviewed)
- Refresh page
- Contact technical support if persists

**Problem: Made a mistake in rejection reason**
- **Solution:** Cannot edit after submission
- Contact nurse directly to clarify
- Document correct information in personal records

## Security & Privacy

### Protecting Patient Information

1. **Access Control**
   - Only access assigned submissions
   - Log out when leaving workstation
   - Never share login credentials

2. **Data Privacy**
   - Do not share patient information
   - Do not screenshot or photograph data
   - Follow clinic privacy policies

3. **Secure Communication**
   - Use secure channels for discussions
   - Do not email patient data
   - Follow PDPA requirements

### Professional Responsibility

1. **Clinical Liability**
   - You are professionally responsible for approvals
   - Exercise independent clinical judgment
   - Document reasoning for decisions

2. **Regulatory Compliance**
   - Follow all applicable regulations
   - Maintain MCR registration
   - Report concerns appropriately

3. **Ethical Standards**
   - Maintain objectivity
   - Avoid conflicts of interest
   - Act in patient's best interest
